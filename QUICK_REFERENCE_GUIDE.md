## üìö UNIT I: Introduction to Research

### 1. Meaning and Types of Research

#### **By Purpose:**
| Type | Purpose | Question | Example |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| **Descriptive** | Describe characteristics | "What is happening?" | Consumer preference survey |
| **Analytical** | Explain relationships | "Why is this happening?" | Analyzing customer churn causes |
| **Applied** | Solve practical problems | "How can we fix this?" | Developing COVID vaccine |
| **Fundamental** | Expand knowledge | "What is the nature of X?" | Studying dark matter |

#### **By Approach:**
| Type | Focus | Data | Sample | Goal |
|------|-------|------|--------|------|
| **Quantitative** | Numbers, statistics | Numerical | Large (100s-1000s) | Generalization, prediction |
| **Qualitative** | Meanings, experiences | Words, images | Small (5-30) | Deep understanding |

#### **By Source:**
| Type | Based On | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Conceptual** | Abstract thinking, theories | Philosophical analysis |
| **Empirical** | Observation, experiments | Clinical drug trial |

### 2. Research Procedures

**8-Step Research Process:**
1. Identify Problem
2. Review Literature
3. Formulate Hypothesis
4. Design Research
5. Collect Data
6. Analyze Data
7. Interpret Results
8. Report Findings

**By Control:**
- **Experimental**: High control, random assignment, can establish causation
- **Quasi-Experimental**: Medium control, no random assignment, natural groups
- **Non-Experimental**: Low control, observation only

### 3. Literature Survey

**Purpose:**
- Establish context
- Identify gaps
- Avoid duplication
- Learn methods
- Build theoretical framework
- Justify research

**Steps:**
1. Define scope
2. Search databases (Google Scholar, Scopus, Web of Science)
3. Use Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT)
4. Evaluate sources
5. Organize (Zotero, Mendeley)
6. Synthesize and write

**Good Research Problem:**
- ‚úÖ Clear and specific
- ‚úÖ Researchable
- ‚úÖ Significant
- ‚úÖ Feasible
- ‚úÖ Original
- ‚úÖ Ethical

### 4. Research Objectives and Hypothesis

**SMART Objectives:**
- **S**pecific
- **M**easurable
- **A**chievable
- **R**elevant
- **T**ime-bound

**Hypothesis Types:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Null (H‚ÇÄ)** | No effect/relationship | "There is no difference between groups" |
| **Alternative (H‚ÇÅ)** | Effect exists | "Training improves performance" |
| **Directional** | Specifies direction | "Higher motivation ‚Üí higher grades" |
| **Non-directional** | Effect exists, direction unknown | "There is a relationship between X and Y" |

### 5. Research Classification

**By Time:**
- **Cross-sectional**: Data at ONE point in time (snapshot)
- **Longitudinal**: Data OVER time (movie)

**By Purpose:**
- **Exploratory**: "What's going on?" (initial investigation)
- **Descriptive**: "What, when, where?" (characteristics)
- **Explanatory**: "Why? How?" (relationships)
- **Predictive**: "What will happen?" (forecasting)

## üìê UNIT II: Research Design & Data Collection

### 1. Research Design

**Components:**
- Research problem
- Research objectives
- Research questions
- Hypothesis
- Variables (IV, DV)
- Population & sample
- Data collection method
- Data analysis plan
- Timeline

**Types:**
- **Exploratory**: Understand new phenomenon
- **Descriptive**: Describe characteristics
- **Causal**: Establish cause-effect

### 2. Questionnaire Design

**Question Types:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Close-ended** | Fixed options | Yes/No, Multiple choice, Likert scale |
| **Open-ended** | Free response | "What do you think about...?" |

**Structure:**
1. Title & Introduction
2. Screening questions
3. Warm-up questions
4. Main questions
5. Sensitive questions
6. Demographics
7. Thank you

**Do's:**
- ‚úÖ Simple language
- ‚úÖ One question at a time
- ‚úÖ Balanced options
- ‚úÖ Pilot test

**Don'ts:**
- ‚ùå Jargon
- ‚ùå Leading questions
- ‚ùå Double-barreled questions
- ‚ùå Double negatives

### 3. Prerequisites of Good Questionnaire

**Characteristics:**
- Validity (measures what it should)
- Reliability (consistent results)
- Clarity
- Relevance
- Appropriate length
- Logical flow
- Neutral wording

**Pilot Testing:**
- Test with 10-30 respondents
- Check clarity, time, flow, missing options

### 4. Data Collection Methods

| Method | Best For | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|----------|------------|---------------|
| **Surveys** | Large samples | Cheap, fast | Self-report bias |
| **Interviews** | Depth | Clarification | Time-consuming |
| **Observation** | Actual behavior | Real behavior | Observer effect |
| **Experiments** | Causation | Control | Artificial |

## üìè UNIT III: Measurement, Scaling & Sampling

### 1. Validity and Reliability

**Validity Types:**
- **Face**: Looks like it measures what it should
- **Content**: Covers all aspects
- **Construct**: Measures theoretical construct
- **Criterion**: Correlates with other measures

**Reliability Types:**
- **Test-Retest**: Same results over time
- **Internal Consistency**: Items measure same thing (Cronbach's Alpha)
- **Inter-rater**: Different observers agree

**Cronbach's Alpha:**
| Œ± Value | Internal Consistency |
|---------|---------------------|
| ‚â• 0.90 | Excellent |
| 0.80-0.89 | Good |
| 0.70-0.79 | Acceptable |
| 0.60-0.69 | Questionable |
| < 0.60 | Poor |

### 2. Measurement Scales (NOIR)

| Scale | Properties | Examples | Analysis |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Nominal** | Categories only | Gender, Religion | Mode, Chi-square |
| **Ordinal** | Order | Rankings, Satisfaction levels | Median, Mann-Whitney |
| **Interval** | Equal intervals, no true zero | Temperature (¬∞C), IQ | Mean, t-test, ANOVA |
| **Ratio** | Equal intervals + true zero | Height, Weight, Age | All operations, ratios |

**Memory Aid: NOIR**
- **N**ominal = **N**ames
- **O**rdinal = **O**rder
- **I**nterval = **I**ntervals equal
- **R**atio = **R**atios meaningful

### 3. Scaling Techniques

**Likert Scale:**
- Most popular
- Measures agreement/disagreement
- Usually 5-point or 7-point
- Example: 1=Strongly Disagree, 5=Strongly Agree

**Other Scales:**
- **Semantic Differential**: Rate between opposite adjectives (Good ‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî Bad)
- **Thurstone**: Equal-appearing intervals
- **Guttman**: Cumulative scale
- **Visual Analog**: Mark on continuous line

### 4. Sampling Methods

**Probability Sampling:**
| Method | How | When |
|--------|-----|------|
| **Simple Random** | Each has equal chance | Homogeneous population |
| **Stratified** | Random from each subgroup | Known subgroups |
| **Systematic** | Every kth element | Available list |
| **Cluster** | Randomly select groups | Geographically dispersed |

**Non-Probability Sampling:**
| Method | How | When |
|--------|-----|------|
| **Convenience** | Whoever available | Pilot studies |
| **Purposive** | Select based on criteria | Expert interviews |
| **Snowball** | Referrals | Hidden populations |
| **Quota** | Meet quotas | Field surveys |

**Sample Size Formula:**
```
n = (Z¬≤ √ó p √ó (1-p)) / e¬≤
```
Where:
- n = sample size
- Z = 1.96 (for 95% confidence)
- p = 0.5 (if unknown)
- e = margin of error (e.g., 0.05)

**Quick Reference:**
- 95% confidence, ¬±5% error ‚Üí n = 385
- 95% confidence, ¬±3% error ‚Üí n = 1,067

## üìÑ UNIT IV: Research Report & Publication

### 1. Primary vs Secondary Data

| Aspect | Primary | Secondary |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Collection** | By researcher | By others |
| **Purpose** | Specific to study | General |
| **Cost** | High | Low |
| **Time** | Long | Short |
| **Control** | Full | None |
| **Examples** | Surveys, experiments | Census, reports, databases |

### 2. Research Report Structure

| Section | Content | % of Report |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Title Page** | Title, author, affiliation | ‚Äî |
| **Abstract** | Summary (150-300 words) | 1-2% |
| **Introduction** | Background, problem, objectives | 10-15% |
| **Literature Review** | Previous research, gaps | 15-25% |
| **Methodology** | Design, sample, instruments | 15-20% |
| **Results** | Findings (tables, figures) | 20-25% |
| **Discussion** | Interpretation, implications | 15-20% |
| **Conclusion** | Summary, limitations, future | 5-10% |
| **References** | Cited works | ‚Äî |

**Citation Styles:**
- **APA**: Social Sciences ‚Üí (Smith, 2020)
- **MLA**: Humanities ‚Üí (Smith 45)
- **IEEE**: Engineering ‚Üí [1]
- **Vancouver**: Medical ‚Üí (1) or ¬π

### 3. Journal Publication Process

**Steps:**
1. Write manuscript
2. Choose journal
3. Format per guidelines
4. Submit
5. Peer review (1-3 months)
6. Editorial decision
7. Revise (if needed)
8. Acceptance
9. Publication

**Decisions:**
- **Accept**: Publish as is
- **Minor Revisions**: Small changes
- **Major Revisions**: Significant changes
- **Reject & Resubmit**: Extensive changes
- **Reject**: Not suitable

**Red Flags (Predatory Journals):**
- ‚ùå Spam emails
- ‚ùå Very fast review (days)
- ‚ùå No editorial board
- ‚ùå Not indexed
- ‚ùå Poor website quality

### 4. Research Metrics

**Journal Metrics:**
| Metric | Source | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Impact Factor** | Web of Science | Average citations per article (2 years) |
| **CiteScore** | Scopus | Citations/publications (4 years) |
| **SJR** | Scopus | Prestige-weighted citations |
| **SNIP** | Scopus | Normalized for field differences |

**Impact Factor Interpretation:**
- IF > 10: Very high impact
- IF 3-10: Good impact
- IF 1-3: Moderate impact
- IF < 1: Low impact

**Author Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| **h-index** | h papers with ‚â•h citations each |
| **g-index** | Top g papers have ‚â•g¬≤ total citations |
| **i10-index** | Papers with ‚â•10 citations |

**h-index Interpretation (varies by field):**
- h > 40: Outstanding
- h = 20-40: Established
- h = 10-20: Active
- h < 10: Early career

### 5. Research Ethics & Plagiarism

**Ethical Principles:**
- Honesty
- Objectivity
- Integrity
- Carefulness
- Openness
- Respect for IP
- Confidentiality
- Responsible publication

**Research Misconduct (FFP):**
1. **Fabrication**: Making up data
2. **Falsification**: Manipulating data
3. **Plagiarism**: Copying without credit

**Types of Plagiarism:**
- Copy-paste
- Paraphrasing without citation
- Mosaic/patchwork
- Self-plagiarism
- Ghost authorship

**How to Avoid:**
- ‚úÖ Always cite sources
- ‚úÖ Use quotation marks for direct quotes
- ‚úÖ Paraphrase AND cite
- ‚úÖ Use plagiarism detection tools
- ‚úÖ Understand what needs citation
